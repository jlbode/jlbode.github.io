Distance from Equator and Weather Patterns

The objective was to visualize the weather of 500+ cities across the world of varying distance from the equator. So as to not schew results, the longitude and latidude were randomly selected for each observations, using values that spanned the entire globe. Since many locations are not cities, the name and location of the nearest city was identified using CityPy, a library with has a method for identifying the city closest to a location. Subsequently, I performed a series of successive API calls to OpenWeather to pull the most recent weather.

Because so much of the earth is covered in Ocean, quite often the closest cities turned out to be coastal, with many of them far north or far south. Duplicates were quite common and were removed.

To better understand where the data was coming from, I used Python gmaps to plot the location of the cities. I used a heatmap to asses if the cities were clustered. As one would expect a heatmap is hotter (more red) when lots of locations are clustered together and colder (greener) when the locations are dispersed. I added popups to provide information about specific observations
The cities in the map were predominantly coastal. However, it was still unclear if we ended up with distorted results simply because most of the population in the world lives in coastal areas. The final list of cities was a plausible representation of cities worldwide. Perhaps the most important finding was that we had ample variation in longitudes, with cities at different distances both north and south of the equator.
